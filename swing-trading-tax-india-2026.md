# Swing Trading — Tax Implications (India, 2026)

---

## How Swing Trading Is Classified

Swing trading (hold 2–10 days, always delivery-based) = **Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG)**.

NOT speculative income (that's only intraday). NOT long-term (need >1 year for that).

---

## Tax Rates That Apply

| Type | Hold Period | Tax Rate |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **STCG — Section 111A** | < 1 year, listed equity, STT paid | **20% flat** |
| **LTCG — Section 112A** | > 1 year, listed equity | **12.5%** (above ₹1.25L exemption) |
| Intraday (speculative) | Same day | Slab rate |

**Swing trading hits 20% STCG. No way around it.**

---

## Critical: Section 87A Rebate Does NOT Apply

Even if total income < ₹12 lakh, **STCG under 111A is taxed full 20%**. Rebate not available. Many traders miss this.

---

## What Gets Added to Tax Bill

| Component | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| STCG tax | 20% |
| STT (Securities Transaction Tax) | 0.1% on buy + sell |
| Surcharge (if income > ₹50L) | 10–15% on tax |
| Health & Education Cess | 4% on tax |

**Effective rate** for most swing traders: ~20.8% (20% + 4% cess).

---

## Loss Rules

- **STCG loss** can set off against STCG or LTCG gains
- **Cannot** set off against salary or other income
- **Carry forward** up to 8 years (file ITR on time — mandatory)
- Loss carry forward only works if ITR-2 filed before due date

---

## ITR Form to File

**ITR-2** — for swing traders (capital gains, no business income).

If you also do intraday → **ITR-3** (business income).

---

## What Records to Maintain

- Contract notes from broker (every trade)
- Buy price, sell price, date, quantity
- Broker's annual tax P&L statement (Zerodha, Upstox provide this)
- STT paid per transaction

---

## Advance Tax Obligation

If total tax liability > ₹10,000 in a year → must pay advance tax.

| Due Date | % to Pay |
|----------|----------|
| 15 Jun | 15% |
| 15 Sep | 45% |
| 15 Dec | 75% |
| 15 Mar | 100% |

Miss this → 1% per month interest under Section 234B/234C.

---

## Budget 2026 — What Changed / Didn't

- STCG raised from **15% → 20%** in Budget 2024 (July 2024). Still 20% in 2026.
- LTCG exemption stays at ₹1.25L (was ₹1L before).
- Budget 2026 made **no rate changes** — only expectation was LTCG exemption hike, didn't happen.

---

## Practical Impact Example

```
Buy 100 shares @ ₹500 = ₹50,000
Sell 8 days later @ ₹550 = ₹55,000
Profit = ₹5,000

STCG tax = 20% of ₹5,000 = ₹1,000
Cess (4%) = ₹40
STT (0.1% each side) ≈ ₹105

Net profit after tax ≈ ₹3,855 (out of ₹5,000 gain)
Effective take-home = ~77%
```

---

## Key Takeaway

Swing trading needs **minimum ~6% move** to be worth it after tax + STT + brokerage. Target 8–10% to actually profit meaningfully.
