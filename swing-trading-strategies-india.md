# Best Swing Trading Strategies — Indian Market (2026)

---

## Core Framework (Ankur Patel's Top-Down Method)

Before any setup, check market first — 70%+ of stocks follow the broad market:

```
Step 1: Market conditions (Nifty trend) → breakouts only work in uptrends
Step 2: Strong sectors (sector rotation scan) → trade leaders in leading sectors
Step 3: Stage 2 stocks → fresh breakout from accumulation base, early in trend
Step 4: Wait for setup (contraction) → enter on expansion with volume
```

**Guiding principles:**
- Follow institutions — join a stock when big money starts buying. Pocket Pivot Volume (up-day volume > highest down-day volume of last 10 days) = best sign of institutional accumulation.
- Contraction → expansion cycle drives every setup below. Tight stocks make the biggest moves.
- Buy early in the trend, not after the big move. "Work with the tide, not against it."
- Pick one lane — master one setup instead of trading everything.

## Strategy 1: EMA Pullback (Most Popular in India)

**Logic:** Stock in uptrend, price pulls back to EMA, institutions reload, bounce happens.

### Setup
```
Trend: Higher highs + higher lows on daily chart
Pullback to: 20 EMA (strong trend) or 50 EMA (deeper correction)
Entry trigger: Bullish candle at EMA — hammer, engulfing, morning star
Volume: Should DROP during pullback (dry volume = supply exhaustion), SPIKE on bounce candle
Stop loss: Below the pullback swing low + 0.5–1% buffer (avoid wick-outs)
Target: Previous high (or 1:2 R:R minimum)
Scanner: Ankur Patel's 20 EMA pullback scan — chartink.com/screener/ankur-patel-s-20-ema-scan
```

### RSI Confirmation
- RSI dips to **40–50 zone** during pullback = healthy, buy the bounce
- RSI dips below 30 = breakdown risk, skip the trade

### Example
```
Stock: ICICI Bank in strong uptrend
20 EMA at ₹1,100
Price pulls back to ₹1,105 on low volume
Bullish engulfing candle forms
Entry: ₹1,110
Stop loss: ₹1,075 (below pullback low)
Target: ₹1,180 (previous high)
R:R = 1:2
```

## Strategy 2: Breakout Trading

**Logic:** Stock consolidates (energy builds), breaks resistance on high volume = big move.

### Setup
```
Pattern: Flat base, triangle, rectangle, cup & handle, VCP, flag, 3-week tight close
Base quality: Linear base > choppy base. Tighter contraction = stronger move
Entry: Candle CLOSES above resistance on 2x+ average volume
Stop loss: Below pivot low of the base + 0.5–1% buffer
           (pivot low = last point buyers showed conviction; break = thesis dead)
Target: Height of pattern projected upward
```

### Volume Rule (Critical for India)
- Breakout without volume = **fake breakout**, skip
- Breakout with 2–3x average volume = **genuine**, enter
- Best confirmation: **Pocket Pivot Volumes** inside the base or on breakout candle (up-day volume > highest down-day volume of last 10 days)
- **Bull Snort** candle = 3x average volume + close in upper 35% of range + above prior close = strongest breakout signal
- Recent red volume (down-day volume above 50-day avg) = unconsumed supply — breakout must take out those days' highs first

### Market Condition Filter (Ankur Patel)
- 70%+ stocks follow the market. Take breakouts only when Nifty is in uptrend — skip them in downtrends.

### Scanners (Ankur Patel's Chartink scans)
- Range contraction: chartink.com/screener/ema-scan-2-7 and chartink.com/screener/new-daily-2045
- 3-week tight close: chartink.com/screener/3-week-tight-close-19 (run weekly)
- Range expansion / volume spike: chartink.com/screener/ankur-s-volume-scan
- Flag patterns: chartink.com/screener/ats-modified

### Example
```
Stock: Tata Motors consolidating between ₹950–₹980 for 3 weeks
Breaks ₹980 with 3x volume on Tuesday
Entry: ₹982
Stop loss: ₹958 (below base)
Target: ₹1,010 (pattern height = ₹30, added to breakout)
```

## Strategy 3: 52-Week High Breakout

**Logic:** Stocks at new highs have NO overhead supply. Institutions chasing performance buy aggressively.

### Setup
```
Stock making new 52-week high
Entry: 1–2% above the high on breakout day
Stop loss: Below the prior consolidation (usually 5–7% risk)
Hold: 5–15 days
Best sectors: Midcap IT, Defence PSUs, Capital Goods
```

### Screener Filter (Chartink)
```
52 week high = new high today
Volume > 150% of 20-day average
RSI(14) > 55
Close > Open (bullish candle)
```

### Ankur Patel's Variant
- Scan stocks trading **within 25% of 52-week high** (not just new highs): chartink.com/screener/copy-copy-52w-1
- Weekend scan: stocks that **close the week at 52-week highs** — closing there, not just touching, = genuine strength
- Leaders live near highs; a true market leader holds up when the market corrects

## Strategy 3B: Episodic Pivot (Ankur Patel's Catalyst Setup)

**Logic:** Surprise catalyst (earnings beat, big order win, policy news) forces fundamental re-rating. Institutions can't buy full size in one day — move continues for days/weeks.

### Setup
```
Trigger: Stock gaps up ~10%+ on massive volume with a genuine catalyst
Entry: Day 1 of the gap (or breakout of day-1 high)
Stop loss: Low of day 1
Best when: Stock was dormant/basing before the news (fresh, not extended)
Delayed EP: Missed day 1? Wait for first tight pullback/consolidation
            after the gap, enter on its breakout
```

Videos: "How to Trade Delayed Episodic Pivot" + "My Scanning Process to Find Delayed EP Setups" on his channel (@AnkurPatel57).

## Strategy 4: RSI Reversal (Oversold Bounce)

**Logic:** Strong stock gets oversold temporarily, snapback is fast and predictable.

### Setup
```
Stock in long-term uptrend (above 200 EMA)
RSI(14) drops below 30 (oversold)
Wait for RSI to CROSS BACK above 30
Entry on that crossover candle
Stop loss: Below the RSI low candle
Target: 20 EMA or previous resistance
```

**Works best in:** Nifty 50 / Nifty 100 stocks only. Avoid midcap/smallcap — oversold can stay oversold.

## Strategy 5: MACD + EMA Combo (High Accuracy)

**Logic:** Two independent signals agreeing = higher probability.

### Setup
```
Daily chart
Condition 1: Price above 50 EMA (uptrend confirmed)
Condition 2: MACD line crosses above Signal line
Entry: Next candle open after both conditions met
Stop loss: Below 20 EMA
Target: 8–10% or when MACD crosses back down
```

### Settings Used by Indian Traders
```
MACD: 12, 26, 9 (default)
EMA: 9, 20, 50
RSI: 14 period
Volume MA: 20-day average
```

## Stock Selection Criteria for Swing Trades

```
ATR/ADR: 2–5% of stock price (enough volatility)
Volume: > 5 lakh shares/day (liquidity)
Market cap: > ₹5,000 crore (avoid penny trap)
Price: ₹100–₹3,000 range (sweet spot)
Trend: Above 50 EMA on daily chart (Stage 2)
Sector: Align with Nifty trend; prefer leading sectors (rotation scan)
```

### Ankur Patel's Dashboard Metrics (AP AI1 tool)
What he checks per stock before entry:
```
% distance from EMA 10 / 20 / 50 (tightness to MAs)
RVOL (relative volume) — today's volume vs average
ADR (average daily range) — stop distance must fit volatility
Distance from 52-week high — closer = stronger
5-week range % — tighter = better setup
50-day avg volume vs current + turnover (liquidity)
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Buying breakouts on **low volume** — fails 70% of time
- No stop loss — one bad trade wipes 5 good ones
- Fighting Nifty trend — individual stock rarely wins vs index direction
- Holding through F&O expiry — unpredictable moves
- Entering at resistance — buy pullbacks, not tops

```
Is Nifty above 20 EMA? → Yes → Look for longs only
Is stock's sector leading? → Yes → Trade the sector leader
Stock above 50 EMA / Stage 2? → Yes → Uptrend intact
Tight contraction + dry volume? → Yes → Setup forming

Stop below pivot low + 0.5–1% buffer → Target = 2x risk minimum
```
