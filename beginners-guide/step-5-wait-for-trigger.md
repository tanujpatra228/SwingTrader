# Step 5 — Wait for the Trigger

You have a watchlist. Now you wait. This step is about patience — you do NOT buy just because a stock is on your watchlist.

You buy only when the stock gives you a clear signal that it is ready to move up.

---

## What is the trigger?

The trigger is called a **breakout.** It happens when:

1. The stock's price **crosses above the top of its resting zone** (the highest point of the tight sideways range from Step 4)
2. On the **same day**, the volume (the bar at the bottom of the chart) is **clearly bigger** than the previous days

Both must happen together. Price move alone is not enough. Volume alone is not enough. You need both.

---

## Why does this work?

When a stock rests quietly and then breaks upward on high volume, it means big buyers (mutual funds, institutions) have decided to buy in large quantities. They cannot hide this — their buying shows up as high volume. When they buy, the price goes up. When they keep buying over days, the price keeps going up.

You are simply joining them.

---

## How to spot it in real time

Each evening, open TradingView and check your watchlist stocks:

Look at today's candle:
- Did price **close above the top of the tight range**? → Possible trigger
- Is today's **volume bar bigger** than the last 5–10 days? → Confirms it

If both yes → this is your buy signal. Go to Step 6 immediately before placing any order.

---

## What does "bigger volume" look like?

Look at the volume bars at the bottom of the chart. During the resting zone, the bars were getting shorter (smaller). A breakout day should have a bar that is **at least 1.5× to 2× taller** than the recent average bars.

If you added Ankur's "Simple Volume with Pocket Pivots" indicator, look for **blue bars** — these mean big buyers stepped in. A blue bar on a breakout day is a very strong signal.

---

## Important: what is NOT a trigger

| Situation | Should you buy? |
|---|---|
| Stock moved up 1–2% but volume is normal | No — too weak, probably random |
| Stock jumped 8% but it was already up 15% last week | No — buying late, too extended |
| Stock crossed the top of the range but on below-average volume | No — not confirmed, could be fake |
| Stock crossed the top AND volume is high | Yes — this is the trigger |

---

## What if you miss the breakout day?

Sometimes the stock breaks out while you weren't watching. The next day it is already 3–5% above where it broke out.

Do not chase it. Do not buy it now.

Either:
- Wait for the stock to come back and rest again for a few days, then try again
- Move on to another stock on your watchlist

Buying late (after the move has already happened) is one of the most common and expensive beginner mistakes. Our backtest proved this: buying the day after a big volume spike → average loss of −0.88%.

---

## How long do you wait?

Some stocks trigger within 2–3 days of going on your watchlist. Some take 2–3 weeks. A few never trigger. That is all fine.

Keep running your daily scans (Step 2) and keep refreshing your watchlist with new setups. Some stocks come off the watchlist (they break down instead of up → remove them). New ones come in. This is an ongoing process, not a one-time thing.
