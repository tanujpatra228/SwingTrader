# How to Screen Stocks for Swing Trading — Beginner Guide

No jargon. Step by step. Start here.

---

## What is swing trading?

You buy a stock, hold it for a few days to a few weeks, sell when it goes up. That's it.

To do this well, you need to buy **strong stocks at the right moment**. Screening = filtering 2,000+ stocks down to a handful worth watching.

---

## Step 1: Check the overall market first (2 minutes)

Open **TradingView** (free) and search **NIFTY 50**. Add EMA indicators (see bottom of this file).

Ask: is NIFTY going up?

- Chart moving from bottom-left to top-right ✓
- Price sitting above the red line (20 EMA) ✓
- Red line (20 EMA) above orange line (50 EMA) ✓

→ All three yes = market is healthy. Go to Step 2.
→ Any no = stop here. Build watchlist but buy nothing. Most stocks follow the market — even great stocks fall when NIFTY falls.

**This one habit prevents most beginner losses.**

---

---

## Step 3: Throw out the junk (2 minutes)

From the scan results, keep only stocks that pass all of these:

- Price above ₹100 (very cheap stocks are risky and easily manipulated)
- Trades a lot daily (high volume column — should be a large number, not tiny)
- You can find the company on Google and it is a real business

Usually you throw out 70–80% of the list at this step.

---

## Step 4: Look at each chart, keep the good ones (10 minutes)

Open each surviving stock on TradingView (daily chart). Ask two questions:

**Question 1: Is it going up overall?**
- Chart should move from bottom-left to top-right
- Price above its 50 EMA line (orange)
- If it's falling or zigzagging wildly → skip it

**Question 2: Is it resting quietly right now?**
- After going up, the best stocks rest sideways in a tight, narrow band for 1–3 weeks
- Price moves barely move — no big swings up and down
- Volume dries up during this rest (volume bars become smaller)
- This quiet zone is called a **base** — like a spring being compressed

You want: big rise → quiet tight rest → that stock goes on your watchlist.

Usually only 3–5 stocks survive. That's perfect.

---

## Step 5: Wait for the trigger (don't buy yet!)

A watchlist stock becomes a **buy** only on the day it breaks out of its quiet zone:

- Price crosses ABOVE the top of the resting range on that day
- Volume bar on that day is clearly BIGGER than recent days (means big money is entering)

**No breakout = no trade.** Some stocks on your list will never trigger. That's fine. You only take the ones that do.

---

## Step 6: Set your exit BEFORE you buy (most important step)

Before placing any buy order, decide these three numbers:

**Stop loss (where you exit if wrong):**
- Find the lowest point of the resting zone (the base low)
- Subtract 1% more as a buffer
- If price falls to this level → sell immediately. No second-guessing.
- Example: base low = ₹500 → stop loss = ₹495

**Position size (how many shares to buy):**
- Decide your maximum loss for this trade (e.g., ₹1,000 or 1% of your account)
- Shares to buy = max loss ÷ (buy price − stop loss price)
- Example: max loss ₹1,000, buy at ₹520, stop at ₹495 → ₹1,000 ÷ ₹25 = 40 shares
- This keeps one bad trade from hurting you badly

**Target (where to take profit):**
- Minimum: 2× what you're risking
- If risking ₹25 per share, target = ₹50 gain per share above your buy price

## Your daily routine (15–20 minutes per evening)

```
1. Is NIFTY above 20 EMA and trending up?
   No → stop, don't trade, update watchlist only

2. Run 1–2 Chartink scans

3. Filter junk (price, volume, real company)

4. Check charts — find stocks in tight bases above rising EMAs

5. Add 3–5 good ones to watchlist

6. Next few days: did any watchlist stock break out on high volume?
   Yes → check stop/size → buy
   No → wait, no FOMO
```

## Common beginner mistakes (from real backtest data)

| Mistake | What happens |
|---|---|
| Buying day after it jumped 10%+ | You buy extended, stock reverses, you lose. Our test: avg −0.88% return |
| Skipping the NIFTY check | Good stocks still fall in bad markets. Most losses happen this way |
| No stop loss before entry | One bad trade wipes out 5 good ones |
| Averaging down (buying more when it falls) | Small loss becomes huge loss |
| Too many stocks at once | Can't monitor, make emotional decisions |

**Also add his volume indicator:**
- Search "Simple Volume with Pocket Pivots" by ank5956
- Blue bars = big buyers stepped in (good signal)
- Red bars = heavy selling happened (be careful)
- Orange bars = very quiet volume (stock resting, compressing)
- Direct link: https://in.tradingview.com/script/JkB0iCFp-Simple-Volume-with-Pocket-Pivots/

**EMA colours to remember:**
- 20 EMA = watch this daily. Price above it = stock is healthy
- 50 EMA = trend line. Price above it = uptrend
- 200 EMA = long-term health. Good stocks stay above it

## Reading NIFTY chart: when to trade vs sit in cash

| What you see on NIFTY chart | What to do |
|---|---|
| Price above 20 EMA, 20 EMA rising above 50 EMA | Full green light — take good setups |
| Price bouncing around the 20/50 EMA (choppy) | Caution — small size or skip |
| Price below 20 EMA and 50 EMA, both falling | Red light — sit in cash, no new buys |
