# Step 6 — Plan Your Exit

A trigger happened (Step 5). Before you place any buy order, you must decide three numbers:

1. Where you will exit if you are **wrong** (stop loss)
2. **How many shares** to buy (position size)
3. Where you will exit when you are **right** (target)

This step takes 3 minutes. Never skip it. Never enter a trade without knowing all three numbers.

---

## Number 1: Stop Loss (what if the trade goes wrong?)

A stop loss is a price level where you agree to accept a small loss and exit the trade. Without it, a small loss can turn into a huge loss.

**How to find your stop loss:**

Look at the resting zone (base) from Step 4. Find the **lowest point** the stock touched during that resting period. That is called the base low.

Your stop loss = base low minus 1%

Why minus 1%? Because sometimes the price dips very slightly below the base low and bounces right back up. The 1% buffer stops you from getting knocked out by that tiny dip. But if it goes more than 1% below — the setup has failed and you should be out.

**Example:**
- Stock rested between ₹490 and ₹520 for 3 weeks
- Base low = ₹490
- Stop loss = ₹490 − 1% = ₹490 − ₹4.90 = ₹485

If the stock falls to ₹485 after you buy → exit immediately. No waiting. No hoping. The setup failed.

**The rule:** stop loss goes at the level where the trade idea is proven wrong. Not at a round number. Not at "whatever feels okay."

---

## Number 2: Position Size (how many shares to buy?)

This is the most important calculation in trading. It decides how much you can lose if the trade goes against you.

**The formula:**

```
Max loss you are okay with ÷ (Buy price − Stop loss price) = Number of shares
```

**Example:**
- Your account: ₹1,00,000
- Max loss per trade: 1% of account = ₹1,000
- You plan to buy at ₹525 (just above the ₹520 top of the range)
- Stop loss: ₹485
- Risk per share = ₹525 − ₹485 = ₹40

Number of shares = ₹1,000 ÷ ₹40 = **25 shares**

Total money used = 25 × ₹525 = ₹13,125 (about 13% of your account)

Even if this trade hits stop loss and you lose the maximum, you only lose ₹1,000 — which is 1% of your account. That is completely manageable.

**Beginner rule:** never risk more than 1% of your total account on a single trade. Some experts say 0.5% when starting out.

---

## Number 3: Target (where to exit when right?)

Your profit target must be at least **2× your risk.** This is called a 1:2 risk-to-reward ratio.

If you are risking ₹40 per share, your target should be at least ₹80 gain per share above your buy price.

**Example continued:**
- Buy price: ₹525
- Risk per share: ₹40
- Minimum target: ₹525 + (₹40 × 2) = ₹525 + ₹80 = ₹605

Why 2×? Because even if you are right only 40% of the time:
- 4 trades win ₹80 each = +₹320
- 6 trades lose ₹40 each = −₹240
- You still make ₹80 profit overall

---

## Placing the trade

Once you have all three numbers, place your buy order:

1. Set a **buy-stop order** at the breakout price (e.g., ₹525) — this order triggers only if price crosses that level, so you don't buy a fake breakout
2. Immediately set your **stop loss order** at ₹485
3. Set a **target/limit sell order** at ₹605

Now you can walk away. The trade manages itself.

---

## Managing the trade after entry

Once the trade is running in your favour, you can **move your stop loss up** to protect profits:

- Stock reaches halfway to target (₹565)? Move stop up to your entry price (₹525) — now you cannot lose money on this trade even if it reverses
- Stock reaches near target (₹595)? Move stop up to ₹570 — lock in most of the profit

**Rule:** only ever move the stop loss upward. Never move it downward to avoid a loss. That turns a small loss into a big one.

---

## When to sell without hitting your stop or target

- Stock runs up very fast in 2–3 days, goes up 15–20%+ rapidly → consider selling some or all. Very fast moves often reverse sharply.
- NIFTY suddenly starts falling sharply → check if your stock is also falling. If yes, consider exiting early.
- You have held for 3 weeks and the stock has barely moved → consider exiting to free up capital for better setups.

---

## Summary: the three numbers before every trade

```
1. Stop loss  = base low − 1%
2. Shares     = (max loss you accept) ÷ (buy price − stop loss)
3. Target     = buy price + (2 × risk per share)

Write these down before clicking buy. Every single time.
```
